The Problem

Why Context Selection Is a Scientific Bottleneck

Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed scientific research — from literature review to hypothesis generation. But a fundamental engineering problem limits their effectiveness: every token in the context window costs compute, money, and attention.

A biotech researcher analyzing 200 protein engineering papers generates approximately 60,000 tokens of abstract text alone. Sending all of this to an LLM means the model processes buffer preparation methods with the same computational weight as a breakthrough finding about enzyme catalytic activity.

Standard RAG retrieves text by vector similarity alone — a paragraph about methodology and a paragraph about a critical mutation mechanism look equally relevant if they share vocabulary. The AI receives both and wastes context on low-value text.

The 7 Semantic Axes

Each axis captures an independent dimension of scientific meaning that vector similarity cannot express:

AxisMeasuresRangeScientific Application
TemporalCurrency of the information-1 → +1Filter outdated findings, prioritize recent results
RelevanceStrategic importance to the query0 → 1Distinguish key findings from supporting methods
RiskUncertainty or threat level-1 → +1Flag preliminary data, unverified claims
OntologyDomain category (Human/Process/Tech/Info/Strategy)0 → 1Classify by subject area within a corpus
CausalityCause vs. effect direction-1 → +1Map causal chains in experimental results
VisibilityPublic vs. internal information0 → 1Control data exposure in sensitive domains
TrustSource reliability0 → 1Weight peer-reviewed vs. preprint findings

Why 7 axes, not 1?

A single relevance score cannot distinguish between (a) a highly relevant, verified, low-risk finding and (b) a highly relevant, unverified, high-risk claim. Both score high on relevance. But one is actionable evidence — the other requires verification. Multi-axis evaluation captures this distinction. Single-score retrieval cannot.

The Tension Formula

A composite urgency score drives automatic prioritization:

Tension = Relevance × Risk × (1 − Trust)

High relevance + High risk + Low trust = Maximum tension → the finding is important, uncertain, and from an unreliable source. It requires immediate attention and verification. This drives automatic triage in literature review pipelines.

Key Insight: Why Completeness Drops

The heuristic evaluator scores chunks by tension (relevance × risk × (1−trust)). Chunks containing specific quantitative details (exact mutation rates, iteration counts) score lower on "risk" and higher on "trust" — because established numerical facts feel neither uncertain nor risky. This causes them to be deprioritized. Solution: add a "specificity" axis that boosts chunks containing numbers, percentages, and measurement units.

Vision

Why Logical Rooms Matters

The AI industry has a context problem. Every major LLM application — chatbots, copilots, research assistants, legal AI — needs to decide which text to send to the model. Today, there are only two options: send everything (expensive, slow, noisy) or use simple similarity search (fast, cheap, but blind to context quality).

The Core Problem

When you ask an AI "What were the mutation rates in this study?", a standard RAG system retrieves chunks that look similar to your question. But similarity is not importance. A sentence describing the experimental setup may be highly similar to your question but contain zero useful data. The sentence with the actual mutation rate (7.2 × 10⁻⁵) may not look similar at all — it's just a number in a results paragraph.

What Logical Rooms Does Differently

Instead of 1 score, we compute 8

Every text chunk gets scored on Relevance, Risk, Trust, Causality, Temporality, Visibility, Ontology, and Specificity. A "mutation rate" chunk scores high on Specificity even if its embedding similarity to your question is low. That's why we keep it.

We select, not summarize

GPT summarization rewrites your text — you lose the original words, can't verify quotes, and introduce hallucination risk. We keep the exact original text. 100% text fidelity, 52% less tokens.

It runs on your laptop

No GPU, no cloud API, no data leaving your machine. The heuristic evaluator processes 4.9 articles/second on a CPU. Patient records, classified documents, trade secrets — everything stays local.

It learns from your corrections

Disagree with a score? Correct it. 10–20 corrections calibrate the evaluator to your domain. The system adapts to your definition of what matters — no retraining, no API calls, instant feedback loop.

Every competitor solves one problem well. LangChain retrieves. ColBERT searches. GPT-4 summarizes. Elicit answers questions.
